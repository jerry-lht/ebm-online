Merge studies into review-level candidates when they refer to the same review-level outcome/comparison.

Prefer benchmark-style canonicalization over abstract-surface copying.
Canonicalization rules:
- outcome_concept: prefer the review-level outcome bucket or canonical concept, not the most specific trial wording. Map concrete instruments, events, or study-specific phrases upward when the SR context supports a broader review-level concept.

- reported_outcome_measures: this is the place to preserve abstract surface forms, instrument names, event wording, and trial-level phrasing.
Anti-patterns to avoid:
- Do not directly promote incidence, episodes, person-time statistics, specific visit windows, or implementation-process wording into the final review-level outcome if the SR context points to a broader canonical meta-analysis setting.

- Do not stop after one vague candidate if the SR context supports multiple canonical review-level outcomes.
Heuristics:
- For continuous outcomes, usually prefer mean difference or standardized mean difference.
- For dichotomous outcomes, usually prefer risk ratio or odds ratio.

Candidate count guidance:

- Do not invent unsupported candidates, but do not under-generate by returning only the most obvious abstract-salient outcome.
