Evaluate whether attrition, withdrawals, exclusions, or missing data could bias the result for outcomes of interest.

Use actual reported numbers about post-randomization attrition on analysed outcomes: per-arm randomized versus analysed, reasons for loss, and time horizon. If those numbers are not in the paper, usually judge Unclear, not High.

Low risk:
- Essentially complete follow-up, loss <= about 10%, no meaningful arm imbalance
- Loss <= about 20%, balanced across arms, reasons not outcome-related
- Appropriate missing-data handling and attrition is moderate
- ITT analysis combined with low or balanced attrition

High risk requires reported numbers establishing bias:
- Total loss >= about 30% on analysed outcome with no convincing missing-data analysis
- Differential loss >= about 10 percentage points between arms
- Loss > about 20% and differential >= about 5 percentage points
- Attrition imbalance with reasons plausibly related to outcome
- Per-protocol/as-treated analysis materially deviating from random assignment

Unclear risk:
- No CONSORT flow and no per-arm analysed numbers
- Only ITT is stated, with no numbers and no flow
- Attrition mentioned but reasons, per-arm split, or size cannot be inferred
