Evaluate whether the person enrolling participants could foresee the upcoming group assignment before each participant was enrolled.

This domain is not about how the random sequence was generated. Computer-generated randomization describes sequence generation, not concealment.

Low risk requires explicit mention of a concealment mechanism:
- Central allocation such as telephone, web-based, pharmacy-controlled randomization
- Sequentially numbered, opaque, sealed envelopes with all three features
- Sequentially numbered identical drug containers
- Other mechanism that clearly prevents foreknowledge
- Electronic or software-based allocation at/after enrolment, including REDCap, web system, tablet/iPad app, interactive voice/web response system, or randomization algorithm embedded in software
- Allocation sequence generated or held by an independent statistician/programmer/data manager, pharmacy, coordinating center, or remote trials unit
- Numbered or preset envelopes opened sequentially after enrolment, especially when the sequence was computer-generated or prepared by someone separate from recruitment

High risk:
- Open random allocation schedule visible to recruiters
- Envelopes without sequential numbering, opacity, and sealing
- Alternation or rotation
- Allocation by birth date, admission date, hospital record number
- Any procedure where recruiter could foresee assignment

Unclear risk:
- Paper describes randomization but says nothing about assignment concealment
- Mentions envelopes without numbering/order or any indication that the sequence was prepared separately from recruitment
- No information about concealment
