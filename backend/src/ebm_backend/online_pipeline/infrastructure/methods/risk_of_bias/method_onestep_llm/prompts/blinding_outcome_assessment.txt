Evaluate whether outcome assessor was blinded to allocation, and whether outcome measurement could be influenced by lack of blinding.

First identify who assessed outcomes: patient self-report, clinician judgement, independent assessor, blinded coder, lab/imaging system, or chart abstractor.

Apply the worst-outcome rule: if the study reports a mix of objective and subjective/patient-reported outcomes and the subjective outcomes are unblinded, the overall judgement is High risk.

Low risk:
- Outcome assessor explicitly blinded and unlikely to be broken
- Assessor unblinded but all outcomes are objective and measurement cannot be influenced

High risk:
- Assessor unblinded or likely unblinded and at least one outcome is subjective or judgement-dependent
- Patient self-report outcomes when participants are not blinded
- Mix of objective and subjective outcomes with no blinding for subjective outcomes

Unclear risk:
- Does not state whether outcome assessors were blinded and outcome types unclear
- Only says double blind without clarifying outcome assessment
